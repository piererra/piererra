Hidden bookmarks.

A locked link is still recognizable as "a link that needs a password," which can look suspicious. Hidden bookmarks disguise it as an ordinary bookmark — clicking it goes somewhere harmless, unless you click a second, companion bookmark right after.

  1. Create a locked link on the main page first, if you haven't already.
  2. Drag the decrypt bookmark below to your bookmarks bar (rename it to something innocuous if you like).
  3. Fill in your locked link and a decoy destination below, then create the disguised bookmark.
  4. To reach the real destination: click the disguised bookmark, then click the decrypt bookmark.

Clicking the decrypt bookmark goes to a decoy page unless the current tab is a disguised bookmark. Set your own decoy below (defaults to Gmail).
